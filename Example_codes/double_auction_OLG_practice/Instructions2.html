  • The structure of the experiment (illustrated below) is as follows:

    • At the beginning of each 3-Period Sequence:
      • Your Type for the sequence, A or B, is randomly assigned and privately communicated to you, so no one else but you knows your Type. Same for every other participant, with both Types being equally likely.
        • {{if info_acquisition == 1}}
      • The State of the World for the sequence, A or B, is randomly determined (with a 50/50 chance) but it is only communicated to some of those who request and pay for it.
        • {{else}}
      • The State of the World for the sequence, A or B, is randomly determined (with a 50/50 chance) but it is only communicated to half the participants.
        • {{endif}} {{if info_acquisition == 1}}
      • Six participants are randomly selected to be "old" members of Generation Zero (G0), they are endowed with {{shares}} shares each and can request to purchase information about the State of the World.
      • Six participants are randomly selected to be "young" members of Generation One (G1), they receive L${{money}} cash and can request to purchase information about the State of the World.
        • {{else}}
      • Six participants are randomly selected to be "old" members of Generation Zero (G0), they are endowed with {{shares}} shares each and half of them are privately informed about the State of the World.
      • Six participants are randomly selected to be "young" members of Generation One (G1), they receive L${{money}} cash and half of them are privately informed about the State of the World.
        • {{endif}}
      • The twelve remaining participants stay "inactive" and partake in a prediction task until called upon.
      • Members of these two generations participate in trading in Period 1.

    • At the end of Period 1:
      • The six "young" members of Generation One (G1) become "old", retaining whatever shares and cash they hold.
      • The six "old" members of Generation Zero (G0) exit the market, get to keep whatever cash they have (which is added to their sequence payoff) and become "inactive" participating in the prediction task until the next sequence.
      • When Generation 0 (G0) exits, any unsold shares (worthless to them) will be distributed randomly to the entering generation at no cost.
        • {{if info_acquisition == 1}}
      • Six "inactive" participants are randomly selected to be "young" members of Generation Two (G2) receive L${{money}} cash and can request to purchase information about the State of the World.
        • {{else}} Six "inactive" participants are randomly selected to be "young" members of Generation Two (G2) receive L${{money}} cash and half of them are privately informed about the State of the World. {{endif}} Members of Generation One (G1) and Two (G2) participate in trading in Period 2.

    • At the end of Period 2:
      • The six "young" members of Generation Two (G2) become "old", retaining whatever shares and cash they hold.
      • The six "old" members of Generation One (G1) exit the market, get to keep whatever cash they have {#after paying back the loan#} (which is added to their sequence payoff) and become "inactive" only participating in the prediction task until the next sequence.
      • When Generation One (G1) exits, any unsold shares (worthless to them) will be distributed randomly to the entering generation at no cost.
        • {{if info_acquisition == 1}}
      • Six "inactive" participants that have not been "active" yet become "young" members of Generation Three (G3), receive {#a loan of#} L${{money}} cash and can request to purchase information about the State of the World.
        • {{else}}
      • Six "inactive" participants that have not been "active" yet become "young" members of Generation Three (G3), receive {#a loan of#} L${{money}} cash and half of them are privately informed about the State of the World.
        • {{endif}} Members of Generation Two (G2) and Three (G3) participate in trading in Period 3.

    • At the end of Period 3, which is the end of the 3-Period Sequence:
      • Dividends are paid to those holding shares only if they are of the same Type as the State of the World regardless of whether they are "old" or "young".
      • Shares held by anyone with a Type different from the State of the World will not pay dividends.
