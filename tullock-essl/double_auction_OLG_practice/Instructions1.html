Welcome to this experiment in decision-making.

  • General Information:
    • Please plan on staying on your computer for the next 120 minutes. If you have any questions let the instructor know.

    • In this session, we conduct a market experiment in which you can trade securities that we will call shares. Each of those shares will pay a certain dividend to its holder at the end of each 3-Period Practice Sequences.

    • You and {{others}} other subjects will participate in {{ num_practice_sequences }} Independent 3-Period Practice Sequences and then {{ num_sequences }} Independent 3-Period Paying Sequences.

    • Instructions will remain the same for every sequence throughout the entire experiment.

    • In each of the sequences you will participate in the market as a "young" and/or "old" active investor only in some, not all, periods. If you do NOT actively participate in the market you will be asked to make predictions about the average price in the market.

    • The process of assignment to the trading roles ("young", "old" or inactive) in the market will be described shortly and trading in each period will last for 120 seconds.

  • Paying Sequences:
    • After the Practice Sequences, you'll enter the Paying Sequences. {# At the end of each of the paying rounds, your payoff (either positive or negative) will be added to your payoffs account.#}

    • At the end of the experiment, a US${{ show_up_fee|to0 }} show-up fee, plus your earnings from one randomly selected Paying Sequence, transformed to US dollars (US$) at a rate of US$1 per L${{us_exchange_rate}}, will be paid to you.

    • If your earnings for the randomly selected Paying Sequence are negative, they'll be rounded up to zero.

    • If you leave before the end of the experiment, you'll be only eligible for the US${{ show_up_fee|to0 }} show-up fee.
